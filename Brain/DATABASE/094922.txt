Photosynthesis is the amazing process by which plants, algae, and some bacteria capture energy from sunlight and convert it into chemical energy they can use to grow and thrive.

It's like their own personal food factory!

Here's a breakdown of the key steps:

  1.

Sun's kiss:   Plants absorb sunlight through tiny holes in their leaves called stomata.

[Image of Stomata on leaf]

  2.

Green magic:   Inside the leaves, chloroplasts contain a green pigment called chlorophyll.

This acts like a trap, capturing the sun's energy.

[Image of Chloroplast]

  3.

Water works:   Plants also take in water through their roots.

4.

Carbon dioxide crunch:   They pull in carbon dioxide from the air through the stomata.

5.

Energy shuffle:   Using the sun's energy captured by chlorophyll, plants combine water and carbon dioxide to make glucose (a type of sugar) in a process called the Calvin cycle.

[Image of Calvin cycle]

  6.

Oxygen output:   As a bonus, plants release oxygen gas as a byproduct.

This is the oxygen we breathe!

The glucose made fuels the plant and can be stored for later.

They also use it to build other molecules like proteins and cellulose.

Importance of photosynthesis:  

    Base of the food chain:   It provides energy for herbivores and indirectly, all carnivores.

Regulates the atmosphere:   Removes carbon dioxide and releases oxygen, crucial for life on Earth.
